EC 3.5.4.28
S-adenosylhomocysteine deaminase

Abstract:
S-adenosylhomocysteine deaminase from bacteria belongs to the amidohydrolase superfamily. Deamination is the main route of S-adenosylhomocysteine metabolism in Streptomyces flocculus ATCC 13257, from where the protein was first described [1]. Now the protein has been identifed in Thermotoga maritima with the help of substrate docking into a 3-dimensional structure solved as part of the structural genomics effort [2]. The docking analysis, and subsequent experimental confirmation showed that S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine, 5'-S-methyl-5'-thioadenosine, and adenosine could be deaminated by the enzyme in a zinc-dependent reaction [2].
