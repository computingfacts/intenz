EC 3.2.1.78
mannan endo-1,4-β-mannosidase

Abstract:
Mannan endo-1,4-β-mannosidase (β-mannanase; EC 3.2.1.78) participate in degradation of hemicellulose and similar polysaccharides by hydrolysing the 1,4-β-D-mannosidic linkages within the main chain of mannans such as galactoglucomannan, the major hemicellulose of softwood. Together with cellulose and lignin, hemicellulose is the most abundant structural component of plant cell-walls. The blue mussel Mytilus edulis produces a β-mannanase (MeMan5A), which is present in the digestive gland of the mollusc. Recently, the crystal structure of MeMan5A has been reported (PDB 2C0H). This is the first three-dimensional structure of a β-mannanase from the animal kingdom. The structure comprises a (βα)8-barrel fold. The enzyme contains an unusually large number (19) of histidine residues, 15 of which are exposed on the surface of the protein. The authors suggest this may relate to its location within the crystalline style (CS) of the digestive tract of the mussel.
