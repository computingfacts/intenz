EC 1.5.3.1
sarcosine oxidase

Abstract:
Heterotetrameric sarcosine oxidase (TSOX) is a bacterial enzyme that catalyses the oxidation of sarcosine by molecular oxygen:

sarcosine + H2O + O2 = glycine + formaldehyde + H2O2

In the presence of tetrahydrofolate, the oxidation of sarcosine is coupled to the formation of 5,10-methylenetetrahydrofolate. TSOX consists of four different subunits. The enzyme contains two flavins per heterotetramer, one FAD attached non-covalently and one covalently attached FMN. The enzyme also contains one molecule of NAD+, the catalytic function of which remains unknown. The three-dimensional structure of TSOX from Pseudomonas maltophilia has been determined at 1.85 Å resolution (PDB:2GAG). The δ-subunit exhibits a new protein fold and, unexpectedly, contains a zinc ion ligated by one histidine and three cysteine ligands.

Reference:

Chen, Z.W., Hassan-Abdulah, A., Zhao, G., Jorns, M.S. and Mathews, F.S. (2006)
