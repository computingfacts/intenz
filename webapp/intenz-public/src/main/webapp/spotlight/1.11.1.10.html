EC 1.11.1.10
chloride peroxidase

Abstract:
Chloride peroxidase (CPO; EC 1.11.1.10) catalyses the halogenation of a number of aliphatic substrates in the reaction:

RH + X– + H+ + H2O2 = RX + 2 H2O

(where RH = substrate, X = Cl, Br, I, but not F). CPO demonstrates remarkable diversity with respect to the reactions which it catalyses. A number of structurally different enzymes have been characterised: CPO from the marine fungus Caldariomyces fumago (CfCPO), which is a heme-thiolate protein; bacterial CPOs without any metal prosthetic group or metal ion (CPO-L); vanadate-containing CPOs; and CPO from the marine worm Notomastus lobatus which represents a flavoprotein:hemoprotein complex.
Although the primary biological function of CPO seems to be chlorination, CfCPO also exhibits peroxidase, dehydrogenase, catalase and monooxygenase activities. The capability of CPO to perform these diverse reactions makes it one of the most versatile of all known heme proteins.
The structures of CfCPO bound with small diatomic or triatomic ligands such as CO, NO, CN– and NCS– have been determined, but so far the co-crystallisation of this enzyme with organic substrates has been unsuccessful. Recently, Kühnel and co-athors have solved the structure of CfCPO complexed with its natural substrate cyclopentane-1,3-dione at a resolution of 1.8 Å (PDB:2CIX). The authors also report structures of CfCPO co-crystallised with either bromide or iodide, and could identify several binding sites, which suggest a pathway for halide access to the heme.
