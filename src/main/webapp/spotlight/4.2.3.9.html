EC 4.2.3.9
Aristolochene synthase

Abstract:
Aristolochene synthase from tobacco belongs to the family of terpene cyclases. Terpene cyclases control the synthesis of cyclic terpenoids including flavours and fragrances such as menthol and camphor, plant defense chemicals like capsidiol and lubimin, and more common compounds like steroids and lipid-soluble vitamins. Generally, these soluble enzymes convert the acyclic isoprenoid diphosphates geranyl diphosphate (10 carbon), farnesyl diphosphate (15 carbon), and geranylgeranyl diphosphate (20 carbon) into cyclic monoterpenes, sesquiterpenes, and diterpenes, respectively. 5-epi-Aristolochene synthase from tobacco (Nicotiana tabacum) converts farnesyl diphosphate into 5-epi-Aristolochene and diphosphate in a magnesium dependent manner. Based on structural studies of the tobacco enzyme it is believed the terpene is formed via two intermediates, germacrene and a eudesmane carbocation.
