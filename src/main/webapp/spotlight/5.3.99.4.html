EC 5.3.99.4
Prostaglandin-I synthase

Abstract:
Prostaglandin-I synthase (PGIS) catalyses an isomerisation of prostaglandin H2 (PGH2) to prostaglandin I2 (prostacyclin), which is a highly potent vasodilator and inhibits platelet aggregation. It is expressed primarily in endothelial and smooth muscle cells, and is associated with the endoplasmic reticulum and the nuclear membrane. This enzyme is a member of the diverse superfamily of P450 enzymes. However, while most of P450s are oxidoreductases requiring an electron donor, PGIS belongs to the subclass of intramolecular oxidoreductases. PGIS does not require any electron-transfer partner or dioxygen. The recently reported crystal structure of human prostacyclin synthase shows that, despite having low levels of sequence identity with other P450 proteins and no redox partners, this enzyme has the same tertiary structure and has a well-conserved heme-binding structural core. A structural comparison revealed that the 3-D structure of PGIS is most similar to that of sterol 14-demethylase (EC 1.14.13.70).
