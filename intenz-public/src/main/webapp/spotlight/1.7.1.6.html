EC 1.7.1.6
azobenzene reductase

Abstract:
Many kinds of bacterial azo dye reductases (azoreductases) have been isolated and characterized for the progress of bioremediation. However, AzoR from Escherichia coli differs from other azoreductases with respect to its requirements for cofactors, electron donors and substrate specificity, as well as amino acid sequence. Curiously, this enzyme was originally thought to be an [acyl-carrier-protein] phosphodiesterase (EC 3.1.4.14). Ito and co-authors have determined the crystal structures of AzoR complexed with FMN in two different crystal forms at 1.8 Å and 2.2 Å resolution. AzoR has a flavodoxin-like structure without the explicit overall amino acid sequence homology, and would likely act as a homodimeric FMN-containing enzyme.

Reference:
Ito, K., Nakanishi, M., Lee, W.C., Sasaki, H., Zenno, S., Saigo, K., Kitade, Y. and Tanokura, M. (2006)
Three-dimensional structure of AzoR (azoreductase) from Escherichia coli: An oxidereductase conserved in microorganisms.
