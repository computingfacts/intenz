EC 1.18.6.1
Nitrogenase

Abstract:
Biological nitrogen fixation, i.e. reduction of dinitrogen to ammonia, is catalysed by the nitrogenase enzyme system (EC 1.18.6.1). Three types of nitrogenase are known, called molybdenum (Mo) nitrogenase, vanadium (V) nitrogenase and iron-only (Fe) nitrogenase [1]. All three types of nitrogenase consist of two components: component I [catalytic subunit or dinitrogenase], and component II [nitrogenase iron protein, or dinitrogenase reductase]. Both components contain iron-sulfur clusters. Mo-nitrogenase, which is found in all nitrogen-fixing organisms, is the best studied enzyme. Nitrogenase also possesses an intrinsic hydrogenase activity. Interestingly, V-nitrogenase uses ~50% of the reducing equivalents for the concomitant production of dihydrogen (eq. 1), whereas Mo-nitrogenase consumes 25% of the equivalents (eq. 2) [2]:
N2 + 12 H+ + 12 e− + 24 MgATP → 2 NH3 + 3 H2 + 24 MgADP + 24 Pi (1)
N2 + 8 H+ + 8 e− + 16 MgATP → 2 NH3 + H2 + 16 MgADP + 16 Pi (2)
It has been suggested that the sequential addition of electrons and protons to the N2 molecule results in a series of intermediates such as diazene (N2H2) and hydrazine (N2H4):
N2 + 2 H+ + 2 e− → N2H2 (3)
N2H2 + 2 H+ + 2 e− → N2H4 (4)
N2H4 + 2 H+ + 2 e− → 2 NH3 (5)
Indeed, it was shown that both hydrazine [3] and diazene [4] are substrates for the wild-type Mo-nitrogenase from Azotobacter vinelandii. The crystal structures of a number of Mo-nitrogenases have been determined. The component I (MoFe protein) is an α2β2 tetramer consisting of two αβ dimers. The MoFe protein contains two types of metal centres, called the M-cluster and the P-cluster. The α-subunit binds the M-cluster, also termed the iron-molybdenum cofactor (FeMoco), which is the substrate-binding site. The P-cluster is bound on the interface of the α- and β-subunits and is a [Fe8S7] cluster. The component II (Fe protein) is a homodimer that coordinates a single [Fe4S4] cluster through two cysteine residues from each subunit.
