EC 4.1.1.37
Uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase

Abstract:
Uroporphyrinogen decarboxylase (UROD) catalyses decarboxylation of all four acetate groups of the tetrapyrrole uroporphyrinogen III to form coproporphyrinogen III. UROD functions in the biosynthesis pathways for heme and chlorophyll and is a special type of nonoxidative decarboxylase, since no cofactor is essential for catalysis. URODs from human, tobacco and Bacillus subtilis have been crystallised and they all reveal that URODs function as homodimers with one active site per subunit, which means the substrate must rotate on the enzyme for the 4 different decarboxylations to take place. Furthermore, the assembly of the dimer juxtaposes the active site clefts of the monomers, suggesting a functionally important interaction between the catalytic centres. The catalytic mechanism is not known in detail, but an invariant aspartate residue (Asp-86 in the human enzyme) presents the only negative charge in the active cleft and as this residue is held in a precise orientation by a hydrogen bond to a nearby backbone amide it is believed to promote formation of a protonated intermediate, which again serves as an electron sink for release of the acetate carboxylate as carbon dioxide.
