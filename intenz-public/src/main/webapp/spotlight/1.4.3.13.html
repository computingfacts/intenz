EC 1.4.3.13
protein-L-lysine:oxygen 6-oxidoreductase (deaminating)

Abstract:
The lysyl oxidase of the yeast Pichia pastoris (PPLO) belongs to the family of copper amine oxidases (CuAO) with a topaquinone (TPQ cofactor derived by post-translational modification of a conserved tyrosine residue. PPLO has the unusual ability to oxidise peptidyl lysine side chains, including lysine side chains in tropoelastin and collagen. The structure of PPLO in a new crystal form has been refined at 1.23 Å resolution. The substrate channel is a broad solvent-filled funnel. Each active site contains a copper(2+) ion, coordinated with three conserved histidine side chains, and a TPQ cofactor. An unexpected intramolecular cross-link has been observed between two lysine residues, Lys-778 and Lys-66. The cross-link is likely to have been formed by the oxidation of Lys-778 followed by a non-enzymatic reaction with Lys-66.
